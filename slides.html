Standard approach

Pearsonâ€™s correlation test revealed that, across 142 participants, variable x was negatively correlated with variable y: \(t(140)=-0.76, p=.446\). The effect size \((r=-0.06, 95\% CI [-.23,.10])\) was small, as per Cohenâ€™s (1988) conventions. The Bayes Factor for the same analysis revealed that the data were 5.81 times more probable under the null hypothesis as compared to the alternative hypothesis. This can be considered moderate evidence (Jeffreys, 1961) in favor of the null hypothesis (absence of any correlation between x and y).

ggstatsplot approach

No more excuses not to explore data! ğŸ˜‰

In summary, the ggstatsplot approach-

(a) avoids errors in statistical reporting,

(b) highlights the importance of the effect by providing effect size measures by default,

(c) provides an easy way to evaluate absence of an effect using Bayesian framework,

(d) demands to evaluate statistical analysis in the context of the underlying data,

and is (e) easy and (f) simple enough that somebody with little coding experience can use it without making an error.
